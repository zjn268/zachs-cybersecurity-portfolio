# zachs-cybersecurity-portfolio

I have worked in information technology since I first started volunteering at a local church providing basic hardware support more than 10 years ago. As I grew in my information technology career, I constantly had a desire to learn more. That desire often led me to digging into how certain ports, protocols, and services work at their core. This led to developing skills across the information technology field from network engineering to DevOps. I became interested in cybersecurity after I completed my CompTIA Security+. The CompTIA Security+ left me with a ton of questions due to the nature of how it hits various cybersecurity topics at their highest level. This only pushed my desire to learn and develop new skills towards cybersecurity since there was so much to learn that could complement all of the other skills I had developed up to that point. Cybersecurity is like an iceberg, what you see above the water line is only the start and what is underwater is miles deep and miles wide with plenty to learn about.
